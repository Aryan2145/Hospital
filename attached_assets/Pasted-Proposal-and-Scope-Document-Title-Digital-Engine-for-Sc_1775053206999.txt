Proposal and Scope Document
Title: Digital Engine for Scalable Growth
Client: Viroc Super Speciality Orthopedic Hospital
Duration: Approx. 4 Months (Can be extended up to 6 months if scope completion extends)
Engagement Type: Mentorship-Led, Hands-On Execution Support

Objective
To support VIROC in establishing a structured, scalable, and system-driven patient acquisition and engagement model that:
Converts awareness into OPD and IPD consistently
Connects digital marketing platforms with a unified CRM and internal workflows
Leverages referrals, education, and after-care programs to build a self-sustaining growth engine
Enables regional expansion through a digitally supported Hub & Spoke model
Equips internal teams with the clarity, tools, and discipline to operate independently
Aligns with VIROC’s long-term growth vision while reducing exposure to external platform disruptions

Scope of Work
RGB will serve as a system architect, trainer, and governance partner. Campaign execution and content creation will remain with VIROC’s internal teams and their selected agencies. Our role is to set up the structure, train the people, document the processes, and ensure sustainability through well-designed systems and reviews.
The engagement will be delivered across seven structured phases. Execution order may be adapted based on VIROC’s team readiness and practical business priorities.


Phase 1: Funnel Mapping and Diagnostic Audit
Map the complete journey: from lead generation to OPD/IPD to referral and follow-up
Review current use of platforms (Meta, Google, WhatsApp, CRM, Email, HMS)
Identify drop-offs and opportunities for automation and process clarity
Define basic funnel metrics and baseline conversion performance
Share a phased action plan that addresses risk, readiness, and internal alignment

Phase 2: CRM System Design and Funnel Structuring
Selection of most suitable CRM tool based on the need
Define lead lifecycle stages: new, consulted, follow-up, converted, referred
Build routing logic to ensure leads reach the right internal contact
Create structured funnels for various sources (ads, referrals, events, webinars)
Set up tracking that’s easy to use and maintain
Keep tool selection flexible until the most appropriate option is finalized

Phase 3: Patient Engagement Automation
Build workflows for nurturing leads, following up with no-shows, and reconnecting with old leads
Automate patient after-care reminders (3, 6, and 12 months post-surgery)
Set up referral coupon tracking systems
Create messaging journeys across patient stages while staying platform-compliant
Use WhatsApp, SMS, and email automation based on approved protocols

Phase 4: Campaign Framework and Lead Activation
Selection of Right Tools for Campaign management and integration across various platforms
Develop campaign strategy structure (to be executed by VIROC team or agencies)
Define tracking, attribution, and performance metrics by region/source
Establish internal workflows for verified number broadcast and outreach
Recommend structure for automated review and testimonial collection
Outline preventive practices to avoid Meta/Google ad policy violations
Suggest contingency plans for platform disruptions

Phase 5: Webinar Engine and Education Funnel
Define webinar strategy including formats, targeting, and scheduling
Set up workflows for webinar registration, reminders, and follow-up
Connect webinar attendees into the CRM funnel for nurturing and tracking
Support development of an education-based trust-building funnel
Provide templates and handover instructions for future internal execution

Phase 6: SOPs, Team Design, and Execution Rhythm
Recommend internal team structure for marketing and follow-up functions
Draft SOPs for:
Lead handling
Referral management
Campaign and communication processes
Provide:
Training decks
Sample scripts
Tracking sheets
Review formats
Set up execution rhythm including:
Daily morning huddles
Weekly progress reviews
Monthly strategic dashboard reviews
Enable task assignment and automated reminders (daily, weekly, monthly) for both internal teams and external agencies

Phase 7: Dashboarding, Reporting, and Transition
Build dashboards to track:
Lead sources
Funnel stages
Referral usage
Campaign ROI
Share weekly and monthly reporting formats
Train internal users on interpretation and usage
Prepare structured handover documentation
Conduct a final implementation check and transition closure meeting

Key Outcomes
By the end of the engagement, VIROC will have:
A clearly structured, easy-to-operate lead-to-patient system
A functioning CRM process integrated with VIROC’s internal team and rhythm
Automated workflows to support patient nurturing and follow-ups
A self-sufficient webinar engine aligned with long-term brand positioning
Documented SOPs and internal processes to ensure consistency
Dashboards and reports that offer leadership visibility
Reduced dependence on external platforms and vendors for core operations
This engagement is designed to be practical, disciplined, and sustainable. Our approach will be modest in promise, abundant in delivery — setting the foundation for bigger goals ahead.
